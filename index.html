Branding for Norda Stelo

After 53 years in the market in 2015, Roche Ltd, Consulting Group became Norda Stelo Inc.
We gave it a brand new face.

My Two Cents

I was in charge of the stationery, the illustrations, the overall style for printed resources, advertising, social media and web. I worked alongside with the other lead designer who created the logo and its style guide, a communication advisor, a project manager and the director of Communications and Marketing Department.

The Guidelines

Having a two-colour palette, our first premise was to use blue for corporate and orange for marketing. The use of straight lines and structures should be considered as a way to express the logical thinking of engineers.

The Approach

Every element stands up as an iteration of the symbol, whose diagonal lines define the edge of the photographs and set a place for the illustrations. No gradients. No curves. We used plain colour, transparencies, vivid photographs and geometrical illustrations, instead. All the illustrations were inspired by the starred sky, as a metaphor of the name, for Norda Stelo means Northern Star in Esperanto.

The Result

The simplicity of the concept and the strong use of colour make the brand memorable and allow the content to stand up. One of our biggest achievements was the smallest change: the use of one single illustration instead of several photographs on the proposal's cover page. It gives a more consistent look and optimizes the time invested on everyday tasks.

Roche's Website

In 2014, the increasing amount of visits coming from mobile devices helped me to convince the team to jump into a responsive website.

My Two Cents

This project was a big task accomplished in a short period of time by a small team: a communication adviser, a copywriter, an integrator and me, a graphic designer. Although my main role was the UI design, I also acted as a UX adviser.

The Guidelines

The main goal was to update the content and the look and feel, while making it available for all web devices. The site was mainly addressed to clients and potential employees.

The Approach

The home page was a window to the whole site and its accent was focused on the projects. All main sections were organized in boxes to make them readable even on a 320px wide screen.

The Result

As expected, Roche's website positioning improved shortly after the shift to responsive was achieved. The site was 100% readable regardless the size of the device. Employees and clients expressed positive impressions on their user experience and the overall look.

Roche's Annual Reports

In 2013, the year of Roche's 50th anniversary, its annual report was presented to the shareholders for the first time on an iPad. Just a few printed copies have been produced ever since then.

My two cents

Based on the tagline provided by the director of Communications and Marketing, I developed the visual concept of each report. I was also in charge of the page layout in French and English, the illustrations and graphics.

The Guidelines

Main texts should be readable without zooming. Each document must be a password protected PDF, personalized with the name of the user.

The Approach and the Result by Year

Some of the content displayed in the following images have been modified given its confidential nature.

2012 - 2013 — Envisioning the Next 50 Years...

Going along with the anniversary theme, I took advantage of the murals I drew for Roche's new headquarters to illustrate the annual report and the visuals related to the shareholders meeting.

The sketches were drawn manually with pencil and ink, then coloured in Photoshop and blended with watercolour brushes made also by myself. This handmade touch balanced quite well the addition of the digital format to deliver the report. It also alluded to Roche's big milestone: half of a century in business.

2013 - 2014 — Building tomorrow together

Visually, this year's approach was to emphasize the human side of the company. The use of typography and colour was bolder and stronger, accompanied by isolated portraits of employees and directors as well as big photographs of the most recent projects accomplished by Roche.

2014 - 2015 — Stay on Course

The market for the biggest firms of engineering in Quebec was hard. So, this year's annual report was all about facing the challenges and getting pass though the storm. All the imagery and colours were chosen according to a nautical theme.

Seasons and Portraits

As days go by and light changes, the way people look at the camera changes, too.

My Two Cents

I'm the one behind the Nikon saying: yes, you are photogenic!

The Guidelines

Although I like to know in advance people's expectations about their portraits, it is always nice to unveil them gradually after a few clicks.

The Approach

Most people dress up for a photographer and those images can indeed be treasured; but to me, the photographs that warm my heart are those that captured the real deal and help us recall the ones we used to be. Because, even on bad hair days, we can find beauty looking back at us from the mirror.

The Result

After a long Canadian winter, Springs brings us hope, love and joy. Summer forces us to go outside and be happy. Autumn makes us fall in love with the light and be thankful for all we have. As for Winter, it can be an invitation to play outside like kids or the best time to cuddle!

OneBigPlanet's Avatars

In 2009, OneBigPlanet Corp. needed some characters created from their logo, a smiley face.
They would be used as avatars for their commerce and loyalty platform.

My Two Cents

Based of the brief provided by the graphic designer of the company, I created the characters.

The Guidelines

Their CEO wanted to add a human touch to the portal. So, he asked for about 15 characters inspired by their logo that users could select to represent themselves while shopping online. They identified the most common profiles of their clients in order to develop the basic avatars.

The Approach

The characters are modular in order to offer the possibility of combining their body parts, clothes, accessories and colours. This allows every user to create their own instance online when using OneBigPlanet's platform.

The Result

19 basic characters were created to represent different kind of users: professionals, students, multiethnic...

Drawing People

These are some of the illustrations I drew back in 2000 for my thesis project about the people in Maracaibo and some sketches I did while commuting to work in Montreal since 2008.

My Two Cents

I draw people for the same reason I photograph them, it's by far my favourite subject.

The Guidelines

For my thesis project, I made the illustrations from photographs taken by myself or my friends and family. The other ones were sketched directly by looking at someone in the subway, bus or train.

The Approach

All sketches were made by hand, using pencils or charcoal. The representation was meant to be realistic, but unfinished, as a drawing study.

The Result

There's something sweet and poetic about drawing people, specially when they aren't aware of your attention.
