Branding for Norda Stelo

After 53 years in the market in 2015, Roche Ltd, Consulting Group became Norda Stelo Inc.
We gave it a brand new face.

My Two Cents

I was in charge of the stationery, the illustrations, the overall style for printed resources, advertising, social media and web. I worked alongside with the other lead designer who created the logo and its style guide, a communication advisor, a project manager and the director of Communications and Marketing Department.

The Guidelines

Having a two-color palette, our first premise was to use blue for corporate and orange for marketing. The use of straight lines and structures should be considered as a way to express the logical thinking of engineers.

The Approach

Every element stands up as an iteration of the symbol, whose diagonal lines define the edge of the photographs and set a place for the illustrations. No gradients. No curves. We used plain color, transparencies, vivid photographs and geometrical illustrations, instead. All the illustrations were inspired by the starred sky, as a metaphor of the name, for Norda Stelo means Northern Star in Esperanto.

The Result

The simplicity of the concept and the strong use of color make the brand memorable and allow the content to stand up. One of our biggest achievements was the smallest change: the use of one single illustration instead of several photographs on the proposal's cover page. It gives a more consistent look and optimizes the time invested on everyday tasks.

Roche's Website

In 2014, the increasing amount of visits coming from mobile devices helped me to convince the team to jump into a responsive website.

My Two Cents

This project was a big task accomplished in a short period of time by a small team: a communication adviser, a copywriter, a graphic designer and an integrator. Although my main role was the UI design, I also acted as a UX adviser.

The Guidelines

The main goal was to update the content and the look and feel, while making it available for all web devices. The site was mainly addressed to clients and potential employees.

The Approach

The interface was based on 12 modules, so that the content could be arranged in one, two, three and four columns. All main sections were organized in boxes to make them readable even on a 320px wide screen. The home page was a window to the whole site and its accent was focused on the projects.

The Result

As expected, Roche's website positioning improved substantially since the shift to responsive was achieved. The site was 100% readable regardless the size of the device. Employees and clients expressed positive impressions on their user experience and the overall look. However, as the company's name and corporate identity changed in December of 2015, this site is not longer available online.

Roche's Annual Reports

In 2013, the year of Roche's 50th anniversary, its annual report was presented to the shareholders for the first time on an iPad. Just a few printed versions have been produced ever since then.

My two cents

Based on the tagline provided by the director of Communications and Marketing, I developed the visual concept of each report. I was also in charge of the page layout in French and English, the illustrations and graphics.

The Guidelines

Main texts should be readable without zooming. Each document must be a password protected PDF, personalized with the name of the user.

The Approach and the Result by Year

Some of the content displayed in the following images have been modified given its confidential nature.

2012 - 2013 — Envisioning the Next 50 Years...

Going along with the anniversary theme, I took advantage of the murals I drew for Roche's new headquarters to illustrate the annual report and the visuals related to the shareholders meeting.

The sketches were drawn manually with pencil and ink, then colored in Photoshop and blended with watercolor brushes made also by myself. This handmade touch balanced quite well the addition of the digital format to deliver the report. It also alluded to Roche's big milestone: half of a century in business.

2013 - 2014 — Building tomorrow together

Visually, this year's approach was to emphasize the human side of the company. The use of typography and color was bolder and stronger, accompanied by isolated portraits of employees and directors as well as big photographs of the most recent projects accomplished by Roche.

2014 - 2015 — Stay on Course

The market for the biggest firms of engineering in Quebec was hard. So, this year's annual report was all about facing the challenges and getting pass though the storm. All the imagery and colors were chosen according to a nautical theme.
