Branding for Norda Stelo

In 2015 after 53 years in the industry, Roche Ltd Consulting Group became Norda Stelo Inc.
We gave it a brand new image.

My Two Cents

Along with the other lead designer, I conceived the stationery, advertising and printed ressources. I also stablished the style of the illustrations, social media and web.

The Guidelines

Our main premise was to use a two-color palette: blue for corporate and orange for marketing. The use of straight lines and structures was a way to recall the logical thinking of engineers.

The Approach

Every composition is an iteration of the logo. Its diagonal lines define placement for the images. We avoid gradients and curves. Instead, we used plain colour, transparencies, photographs and vectorial illustrations. The starred sky is a metaphor of the name, as Norda Stelo means Northern Star in Esperanto.

The Result

The simplicity of the concept and the strong use of colour makes the brand memorable and allows the content to stand out. One of our biggest achievements was coincidentally the smallest change: the use of a single illustration instead of several photographs on the proposal's cover page. It gives a more coherent look and optimizes the time invested on everyday tasks.

Roche's Website

In 2014, the amount of visits coming from mobile devices got us into the task of creating a responsive website.

My Two Cents

This project was a big task accomplished in a short period of time by a small team. Although my main role was the UI/graphic design, I also acted as a UX adviser.

The Guidelines

Our goal was to update the content and style of the website while making it available for all web devices. The site was mainly geared towards clients and potential employees.

The Approach

The home page was a window to the whole website and its accent was focused on the projects. All main sections were organized in boxes to make them readable even on a 320px wide screen.

The Result

As expected, Roche's website positioning improved shortly after the shift to responsive was achieved. The site was 100% readable regardless the size of the device. Employees and clients expressed positive impressions on their user experience and the new style.

Roche's Annual Reports

In the year of Roche's 50th anniversary, the annual report was presented for the first time on an iPad. Only a few printed copies have been produced ever since.

My two cents

Based on the tagline provided, I developed the visual concept of each report. I was also in charge of the page layout in French and English, the illustrations and the graphics.

The Guidelines

Main texts had to be readable without zooming. Each document had to be a password protected PDF, personalized with the name of the user.

The Approach and the Result by Year

Some of the following content has been modified given its confidential nature.

2012 - 2013 — Envisioning the Next 50 Years...

To illustrate the annual report, I used some of the drawings I made for Roche's new headquarters. It balanced quite well the addition of the new format to deliver the report. It also alluded to Roche's big milestone: half of a century in business.

2013 - 2014 — Building tomorrow together

This edition emphasized the human side of the company and its achievements thought photographs. The use of typography and colour was bolder and stronger.

2014 - 2015 — Staying on Course

During the last years, the industry of engineering firms in Quebec did not only get more complex, it also had to face many difficulties. This is why the message conveyed by this year’s annual report intended to showcase a capacity to face challenges and brave all storms.

All the imagery and colours were chosen according to a nautical theme.

Seasons and Portraits

As days go by and light changes, the way people look at the camera changes, too.

My Two Cents

I'm the one behind the Nikon saying: yes, you are photogenic!

The Guidelines

Although I like to know in advance people's expectations about their portraits, it is always nice to slowly discover them after a few clicks.

The Approach

Most people dress up for a photographer and those images can indeed be treasured, but to me, the photographs that warm my heart are those that captured reality and help us remember the ones we used to be. Even on bad hair days, we can find beauty looking back at us from the mirror.

The Result

In spring, a portrait must be taken outside, for none wants to be trapped between walls after a long winter. Same thing goes for a summer portrait, although people are even happier and more playful. Fall is the golden time for this subject, as the light is soft and magical. Winter can be both jolly and nostalgic, depending on where the session is performed.

Everyday Life

A non-ambitious depiction of little things that would probably be forgotten without photographs.

My Two Cents

These are some of my personal tales.

The Guidelines

To preserve some memories of our everyday life.

The Approach

Three images, one story.

The Result

Five short stories told from a general plan to a small detail.

Autumn in the country

I thought autumn was gorgeous the first time I experienced it in the city. Then, my friend took us to the countryside and I realized that I knew absolutely nothing about it. This first capture was taken from the moving car.

A 30-degree day in March

Something odd happened with the weather by the end of March 2012: we had a taste of summer for almost a week. Usually by this time of the year in Montreal, the snow is everywhere and when it falls, the flakes look big, light and fluffy. It is normally very cold, but not that day. So, we spent the afternoon happily barefoot in the park.

Ottawa's Tulip Festival

A two-hour trip to enjoy one of the most colourful palettes that spring can offer.

An Afternoon walk

Back when we lived near downtown and the days were long, Sam and I used to go for a walk before the sunset. Then and there, Montreal's Victorian architecture swept me off my feet.

Grandma in Exporail

A lovely 85-year-old lady travelled from Venezuela, all alone, to spend the summer with us. This is what happened when my little boy took us to his favourite place on Earth: Exporail, the Canadian railway museum.

OneBigPlanet's Avatars

In 2009, OneBigPlanet Corp. needed some characters created from their logo, a smiley face. They would be used as avatars for their commerce and loyalty platform.

My Two Cents

Based on the summary provided by the graphic designer of the company, I created the characters.

The Guidelines

Their CEO wanted to add a human touch to the portal. He asked for about 15 characters inspired by their logo that users could select to represent themselves while shopping online. They identified the most common profiles of their clients so that I could develop the basic avatars.

The Approach

The characters are modular, meaning the user can have the possibility of combining body parts, clothing, accessories and colours. This allows every user to create their own online persona when using OneBigPlanet's platform.

The Result

19 basic characters were created to represent different kind of users: professionals, students, multiethnic...

Drawings of People

These are some of the illustrations I made back in 2000 for my thesis project about the people in Maracaibo and some sketches I did while commuting in Montreal since 2008.

My Two Cents

I draw people for the same reason I photograph them, it's by far my favourite subject.

The Guidelines

For my thesis project, I made the illustrations from photographs taken by myself or my friends and family. The other ones were sketched directly by looking at someone on the subway, bus or train.

The Approach

All sketches were made by hand, using pencils or charcoal. The representation was meant to be realistic, but unfinished, as a drawing study.

The Result

There's something sweet and poetic about drawing people, especially when they aren't aware of your attention.
